About

Research-Oriented Profile

I am a master's student at Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications, majoring in Control Science and Engineering. My current work is situated at the intersection of multimodal large models, cross-modal generation, and uncertainty evaluation.

I am especially interested in how text-to-image models express, amplify, or conceal uncertainty when prompts involve ambiguous semantics, domain-specific visual concepts, or scenario-dependent generation requirements.

Research

Research Focus

Cross-Modal Generative Uncertainty

Quantifying uncertainty that emerges when multimodal models translate textual conditions into visual outputs across different semantic and visual scenarios.

Text-to-Image Model Evaluation

Designing evaluation protocols for text-to-image foundation models, with emphasis on uncertainty, semantic alignment, and scenario-level robustness.

Multimodal Large Models

Studying how multimodal model behavior can be measured beyond surface-level generation quality, especially when outputs are plausible but not reliably grounded.
